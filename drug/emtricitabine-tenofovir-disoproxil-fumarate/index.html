PBAC's latest decision on Emtricitabine + tenofovir disoproxil fumarate: Not recommended (2016). Considered for Pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP) to reduce the risk of sexually acquired HIV-1 infection in adults at substantial risk of HIV infection.

Eligible population

Adults aged 18 years or older at substantial risk of sexually acquired HIV-1 infection (defined as annual incidence of 3% in absence of PrEP), who return negative HIV-1 test prior to and within 7 days of initiating treatment, enrolled in an approved PrEP service and treated by an ART-approved prescriber

Why PBAC said no

Reasons cited in the latest PSD: Unreliable cost-effectiveness estimates, arbitrary 3% annual incidence threshold not justified, risk calculator does not exist for Australian population and would be based on unreliable self-reported behaviour, inability to accurately estimate future individual risk, proposed eligibility restrictions not practical or appropriate
