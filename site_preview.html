What this means

For most drugs reviewed by both committees, NICE moves first — often by six months to two years. In rare disease, where evidence bases are thin and prices high, the gap is worst.

Australia is not uniformly behind: for some cardiovascular and diabetes drugs, PBAC listed first. The pattern that emerges is one of structural divergence in oncology and rare disease, rather than a blanket access problem.

This analysis is drawn entirely from publicly available documents and is free for clinicians, patient advocates, and researchers.
